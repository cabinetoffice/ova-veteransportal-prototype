Get emergency financial help

If you’re having a financial emergency, there are different types of help including:

  • crisis grants
  • other benefits
  • specific grant or support for your needs, for example money that must be spent on something specific like furniture or food vouchers

Crisis grants

Crisis grants are payments given in financial emergencies. You do not need to pay them back.

Crisis grants have different names and you apply for them in different ways depending on where you live. If you live in:

  • England - it's called the ‘Household Support fund’ and is available from your local council
  • Scotland - it's called the ‘Scottish Welfare Fund’ and is available from your local council
  • Wales - it's called ‘Emergency Assistance Payments’ and is available from the Welsh Government
  • Northern Ireland - it's called ‘Discretionary Support’ and is available from the Northern Ireland Government

Other benefits

If you're having a financial emergency, you may be entitled to benefits. If you're already claiming benefits, you may be entitled to extra money during your financial emergency.

Check what benefits you're entitled to.

Specific grant

If you need help with a specific expense, there may be help available from other organisations. For example, if you need help with debt repayment or to buy furniture.
